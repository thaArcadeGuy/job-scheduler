Why we chose the heap

The timing wheel wins on raw throughput in 5 of 6 scenarios — its O(1) insert is hard to beat for large volumes of same-range jobs. But our scheduler has two requirements the timing wheel can't satisfy cleanly:

1. Priority ordering. Jobs must process in priority 1 → 2 → 3 order. The wheel has no inherent ordering within a slot — a secondary sort would be needed on every tick.

2. Precise backoff timing. Retries fire at ~1s, ~5s, and ~25s with ±jitter. The wheel's fixed 1s resolution loses sub-tick precision. The heap handles arbitrary delay values natively.

The one scenario where heap wins — wide range 0–600s (forced overflow) — is exactly the real-world retry case. At our expected job volumes the log(n) factor is negligible.
